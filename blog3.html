All Done, Now What?

  • 11 June, 2018
  • Web Development

I always love the feeling of completing something that I have worked so hard on. Well, on Friday, I graduated from my web development immersive class. It still feels a little surreal. I was working nearly night and day for twelve weeks, barely taking a break from my computer screen and now I actually have a little free time. It feels like it both, took forever and went by in the blink of an eye. I have learned so much in such a short time that it may take another couple days for it to really sink in that I have actually finished.

The immersive program was one of the most intense learning experiences I have ever had. It was challenging almost the whole time and required me to learn whole new coding languages and libraries in a week or less for each. I learned so much more during project weeks than I did during the direct instruction, but isn’t that the way it usually goes? I have always been more of a hands-on learner. It was all worth it though, when I first thought of going this route, I worried that I wouldn’t like to code. It did not take very long for me to realize just how much I love it. I feel a little ridiculous for taking this long to get to it, really.

Now another journey begins, that of finding a job. I know it may seem tedious at times, but once I get my foot in a door, I get to follow the real dream I have of being a web developer. I have a lot of work ahead of me, but once I manage to land my first web dev role, I will be on my way to having the life I have been dreaming of. If you can’t tell, I am beyond excited to get this whole job hunt thing over with and get on the the good parts.

Wish me luck, I will keep you posted. Before too long, I will be getting paid to do something I truly enjoy.
